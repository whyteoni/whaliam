## Who/What is a Whaliam Wallace?

Many years back now, somewhere in the early 2010s, I had a thought pop in my head. This was a time of vampires, werewolves, and increasingly other were-beasts. _Underwold_ and _True Blood_ had not quite given way to _The Walking Dead_ and _Game of Thrones_ or _Stranger Things_. The increasing pantheon of were-beasts (werecats, werebears, and so on) lead to an obvious fascination, "What other creatures could be given the _were-_ treatment?" And in the tempest of that context I had an image resolve in my head: a werewhale. More specifically I had an image of a were-humpback-whale (becuase _Star Trek 4: A Voyage Home_ was my go to movie as a kid). It was in the ocean, racing to the shore, trying to outrun the sun as it crested the horizon behind it. The werewhale had already begun to change, and his powerful fluke had returned to the naked legs of a man, massively disproportionate to the rest of the body. He wasn't going to make it.

This paradoxical creature entrigued me. In the fashion of a _reductio ad absurdum_ inquest I conjured scenarios that, to me, seemed hilarious, and also tested the theoretical bounds of the _were-_ mythos. What happens if a were-beast dies while transformed? Do their remains stay in same form the person/create was in at the moment of death? Are there were-humans, beasts who turn into humans during the full moon? These and many more questions and their, often grotesque, answers became a recurring theme in the random spaces the mind wastes time in when the Wi-Fi is bad in the doctor's lobby.

Of all the beasts imagined though, none stayed with me more than the first, the werewhale I named William, no... _Whaliam_. And if you are a William fighting for the survival of your kind, mythical or otherwise, you might as well be a Wallace, and thus was crafted the idea of _Whalium Wallace_.
